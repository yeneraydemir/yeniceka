How to Build a Flow Plan for Eid Slaughter Lines

November 18, 2025

A flow plan is the first step to prevent bottlenecks and keep operators safe on an Eid slaughter line. Position bleeding, hide removal, organ separation and deboning so each step feeds the next. The goal is a seamless flow between cattle lines, small ruminant solutions and deboning equipment.

Line Layout

  • Place the bleeding line closest to entry where drainage is strongest.
  • Plan hide removal stations with non-slip platforms and safe locking equipment.
  • Separate organ lines from the deboning line with washable surfaces.
  • Align deboning and weighing stations parallel to the cold room transfer.

Capacity and Synchronization

Balance operator counts and equipment capacity at every station. Sync rail speed and platform heights to lower error rates. Match transfer platform and conveyor speeds to the cold room staging rail capacity.

Checklist

  • Are rail speed and platform height synchronized?
  • Is there zero return traffic between the organ line and deboning line?
  • Are hygiene barrier entries sized for capacity?
  • Is cold room drainage and staging rail capacity sufficient?

Frequently Asked Questions

How should I calculate line capacity?

Set an hourly slaughter target, distribute it evenly across bleeding, hide removal and deboning stations, then add a 20% safety margin.

If a bottleneck appears, where do I start?

Check rail speed and platform height first, then shorten the wait on the deboning-weighing-cold room bridge.

How should I position the organ line?

Place it apart from the deboning line on washable surfaces with drainage, and keep it close to knife sterilization.
