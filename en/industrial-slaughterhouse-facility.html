Industrial Slaughterhouse

Integrated facility, balanced capacity and sustainable operation

An industrial slaughterhouse is a modular system connecting every step from reception to cold chain. Each correctly scaled module is critical for hygiene and efficiency.

In an industrial-scale slaughterhouse, line design is shaped by capacity targets, product mix, shift structure and cold room configuration considered together. Rail geometry, hygiene barrier positioning and automation point selection directly determine annual carcass volume.

At Ceka Abattoir we begin with a site survey; complete the engineering file with flow diagrams, layout plans and equipment lists; then manage manufacturing, assembly and commissioning with the same team. This delivers measurable capacity, consistent hygiene standards and low maintenance costs.

Installation Process

Industrial facility in 6 steps from survey to commissioning

Every phase is executed by the same team; engineering decisions are validated on-site, field data feeds back into engineering.

01

Survey & Analysis

Site measurement, capacity target and existing infrastructure analysis. Logistics and waste management assessed.

02

Engineering & Design

Flow diagram, layout plan, rail calculations and equipment list. 2D/3D files.

03

Manufacturing

Stainless steel fabrication in our own workshops, weld tests and factory acceptance test (FAT).

04

Logistics & Installation

Equipment delivery to site, rail and platform installation, electrical/pneumatic connections.

05

Commissioning

No-load tests, capacity trials, hygiene and safety checks. SAT approval process.

06

Training & Support

Operator training, maintenance plans and 24/7 technical service. Spare parts supply guarantee.
